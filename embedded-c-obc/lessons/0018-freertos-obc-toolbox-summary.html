Lesson 0018: FreeRTOS OBC Toolbox Summary

This lesson is not about memorizing every FreeRTOS primitive. It is about choosing the smallest primitive that matches the boundary you actually need.

The Decision Habit

Start with the firmware problem, not the primitive. Ask what must be preserved: a wakeup, a bit of state, every sample, mutual exclusion, or a fixed schedule.

OBC habit: primitive choice follows ownership. First decide who owns state, who runs, and what crosses the boundary.

Use Sparingly For Now

Mutexes, binary semaphores, event groups, timers, stream buffers, message buffers, critical sections, atomics, and static allocation are all real tools. They are not the first answer for every OBC problem.

Review smell: choosing a mutex before asking who should own the data often creates avoidable concurrency work.

Scenario Drill

1. The barometer interrupt should wake exactly one task to read a status register. Repeated wakeups can coalesce.

2. The IMU producer must preserve each sample until the flight task receives it.

3. Two tasks want to update one health struct directly.
