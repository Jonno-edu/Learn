Lesson 0007: Nominal Attitude Prediction

Gyro measurements update the nominal quaternion. This lesson turns angular rate over a small time step into a quaternion increment.

The One Thing

The corrected body angular rate is integrated over dt_s: 

dtheta_b = omega_b * dt_s

That small rotation becomes a delta quaternion, which is composed with the current q_nb. The result is normalized before returning.

Small Rotation To Quaternion

For small angles, a useful first implementation is: 

angle = norm(dtheta_b)
axis  = dtheta_b / angle
dq    = [cos(angle/2), axis_x*sin(angle/2), axis_y*sin(angle/2), axis_z*sin(angle/2)]

If the angle is extremely small, use the identity quaternion or a small angle approximation to avoid dividing by zero.

Quick Check

Why normalize after attitude prediction?

Integration and floating-point multiplication can move the quaternion away from unit length. Normalization keeps it valid as a rotation.
